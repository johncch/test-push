Once upon a time, a curious wanderer stumbled upon a hidden library at the edge of the world, its shelves stretching up into clouds no eye could see. Every book held a story that had never been told, waiting patiently for the right reader to set it free.

The wanderer walked through the bookshelves, tall as anything can be, and filled with books of all colors, shapes and sizes. She didn't know which to pick.

Then, as if by magic, a single small book tumbled from a high shelf and landed open at her feet, its pages glowing with a faint golden light. She knelt down and read the first line, and in an instant she felt the story reach out and take hold of her heart. There was nothing left to do but sit down on the cool stone floor and read until the clouds above her turned pink with dawn.

It was as if she was sucked into another world. As she flipped through the pages, the words jumped out at her and engulfed her reality, transforming the bookshelves around her into another scene. There were rolling emerald hills beneath a violet sky, and a silver river winding toward a city whose towers chimed softly like bells in the wind. She walked forward without thinking, the book still open in her hands, and realized with a quiet wonder that she was no longer reading the story — she had become it.
